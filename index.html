Eco-Transformers: Carbon-Efficient and Accelerated Inference through Self-Pruning and Activation Freezing in Large Language Models

Sivanesan B, Shriaarthy E, Sangeetha S, Niranjana R S, Rohini R, Vijayalakshmi N

Published In: Third International Conference on Next Generation Computing Systems (ICNGCS - 2025)

Location: Coimbatore, India

Date: 21-22 August 2025

Large Language Models (LLMs) are getting larger and more complex, with increased computational requirements, latency, and carbon footprint—challenges that render AI deploy- ment unsustainable and inefficient. In this paper, we introduce Eco-Transformers, a novel runtime optimization technique for improving Transformer model inference efficiency. The technique dynamically removes low-magnitude weights by masking and freezes neuron activations near zero. Crucially, these optimiza- tions are done at runtime—without retraining or altering the model structure. With PyTorch forward hooks implementation, the framework is lightweight, architecture-independent, and completely compatible with real-time and low-resource environ- ments. Eco-Transformers was tested on four transformer models: Falcon-RW-1B, GPT-Neo-1.3B, OPT-1.3B, and GPT2-XL. Eco- Transformers consistently reduced CO2 emissions and inference latency across all tested models while maintaining output quality as verified by BLEU, ROUGE-L, and perplexity scores. Of these, GPT2-XL saw the greatest inference efficiency and carbon sav- ings enhancement, and GPT-Neo-1.3B was the most sustainable in absolute terms. These findings validate Eco-Transformers as a cost-effective and efficient method for low-latency and carbon- efficient inference in transformer-based language models.
